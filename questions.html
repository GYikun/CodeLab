Research Questions

One idea sits behind all of our work: the brain's real behaviour shows up in the small, unremarkable things — a tap, a swipe, a pause before unlocking the phone. These are the questions we ask of that behaviour.

Explanation

How does the brain process everyday smartphone interactions?

We study the neural correlates of goal-directed and idle movements on a touchscreen, linking specific brain activity to specific taps and swipes.

Can smartphone behaviour reveal how we age?

Healthy ageing leaves a signature in the rhythm of everyday touchscreen use — one that stays remarkably stable even as other measures change.

Can smartphone data catch neurological disease early?

Behavioural-age models and AI trained on ordinary phone use can flag epilepsy and other neurological conditions, sometimes before symptoms are obvious.

What can smartphones tell us about sleep?

Combining touchscreen activity with wearables and self-reports lets us trace sleep-wake cycles and stable, trait-like nighttime sleep patterns.

Are there hidden rhythms in everyday behaviour?

Smartphone interactions follow shared rhythms that repeat across multiple days — a multi-day structure to behaviour most of us never notice.

Can this data power new digital health tools?

Our discoveries have already seeded two spin-off companies, translating findings about the brain and behaviour into tools clinics can use.
